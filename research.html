Research

3D Boundary Element Methods

 I am developing computational tools for boundary integral representation of solutions of wave problems. This also includes scattering on sound-soft obstacles. Currently I am improving our team’s MATLAB library by including Laplace domain solutions of these problems using single and double layer potentials.

Numerical Analysis of Viscoelastic Waves

 The stress-strain relation of viscoelastic materials can be modeled using classical as well as fractional Zener models. We present properties of these models and the assumptions needed to investigate waves in viscoelastic media. Under external load and traction terms we study the stability and well-posedness of the viscoelastic wave problem in both the Laplace and time domains. We then examine the semidiscrete problem and analyze the error. We use our team’s computational package to test our theoretical results on a problem with the fractional Zener model and create three dimensional simulation of the solution. Below is the result of one of our numerical simulations where the material is modeled by Fractional Zener model and the bottom of the solid is shaken but the rest of the boundary is free. The traveling viscoelastic waves from bottom to top as well as the dissipativity of the system are captured.
