About

Doctoral candidate at Department of Mathematical Sciences, University of Delaware. I am supported by the University of Delaware via the dissertation fellowship award for the academic year 2019-2020. My anticipated graduation date is May, 2020.

Up until April, 2019, I was a proud member of Team Pancho supervised by Dr. Francisco Sayas. Unfortunately Dr. Sayas (aka Pancho) lost his battle with cancer. I am currently under the supervision of Dr. Peter Monk.

Interests

  • Scientific computation. High order numerical approximations of solutions of partial differential equations. This includes elastic and viscoelastic wave propagation problems.
  • Computer vision and object segmentation. This has two sides. Usage of classical tools, more specifically, implementing active contour models and solving energy minimization problems. Secondly, making use of modern visual recognition systems based on deep learning.
  • Software and algorithm design. Implementing fast, vectorized, parallelized, object-oriented, and well-documented software. I have been contributing to a couple of team-projects, and the products will be publicized soon.

Tools

Here is a list of tools that are often used in my research and the products I design.

  • Python, MATLAB, NumPy/SciPy, FEniCS/DOLFIN, Object Oriented Programming, Git
  • Numerical Analysis, Finite and Boundary Element Methods, Continuous and Discontinuous Galerkin Methods, Elliptic PDEs, Viscoelasticity, Scattering Theory
  • Image Segmentation, High Performance Computing, Software and Algorithm Development, Machine Learning
